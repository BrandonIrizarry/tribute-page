Bjarne Stroustrup

I decided to design my FreeCodeCamp Tribute Page around the Danish computer scientist Bjarne Stroustrup, the original designer and implementer of C++. C++ is arguably one of the most influential computer languages that have ever been designed: according to the C++ Wikipedia page, it has influenced the design of Python, Java, Rust, and even C99 (a version of C that had come after C++). It also boasts ubiquitous industrial applications.
Piggybacking off of the elegance and efficiency of the C programming language, C++ is truly a one-of-a-kind product: it is a meeting place of expressivity and efficiency.
Stroustrup maintains an interesting presence on the Web via his personal webpage. Here's a quick timeline of Stroustrup's accomplishments:
  • Born December 30, 1950, in Aarhus, Denmark
  • Attended Aarhus University 1969â€“1975 and graduated with the Danish equivalent of a good master's degree in mathematics and computer science.
  • Received in 1979 a PhD in computer science from the University of Cambridge, for a thesis on communication in distributed computing systems. He was supervised by David Wheeler.

    David Wheeler is considered a co-inventor of the subroutine technique in computer programming, something we all but take as an elementary principle for quite some time now.
  • Also in 1979, joins the technical staff at the Bell Labs Computer Science Research Center, the famous birthplace of C and UNIX. There, he began his work on C++ and programming techniques.
  • From 2002 to 2014, Stroustrup was the College of Engineering Chair Professor in Computer Science at Texas A&M University. From 2011, he was made a University Distinguished Professor.
  • As of January 2014, Stroustrup is a technical fellow and managing director in the technology division of Morgan Stanley in New York City and a visiting professor in computer science at Columbia University.
